Sovereign AI in India · 2026

Sovereign AI Thesis for India

India already delivers identity, payments and benefits through its own digital systems — Aadhaar alone has issued over 1.44 billion IDs (IBEF / PIB, 9 Mar 2026). No country builds all of AI itself, so own the parts that decide what citizens get and rent the rest.

  • Getting AI to people is where India can win. Not money or chips, but its own languages, citizen data and delivery — which needs a model built in India, not a foreign API or a re-skinned foreign model.
  • The customer has no choice — it isn't a sale. In government and in banking, the rules already shut out top foreign models: RBI's committee recommended banks build an Indian finance model (Dvara Research — RBI FREE-AI, 13 Aug 2025), atop a 2018 rule that payments data must stay in India (RBI, 6 Apr 2018), and the new personal-data rules push the same way (DPDP Rules, 14 Nov 2025).
  • Each win makes the next one easier. One required customer, delivered hands-on, is the proof that wins the next regulated one — and adds more data and reach.
Money is in US$; rupees convert at US$1 ≈ ₹84, the 2025 average (site convention; IndiaAI Mission ₹10,371.92 cr ≈ US$1.23B).

  • A capital-short country can own deployment sovereignty: own the language, citizen data and delivery, rent the chips, say so.

The sectors to play

Enter only where the State or regulator bars a foreign model by law, not where you could just out-compete one. Two sectors are wide open; the rest are conditional, one way in each.

Government & Public DPI

widest open space

A foreign model on citizen data is a non-starter

The opening
  • No Indian-language sovereign agent runs the citizen-service and benefit systems yet.
  • The State owns all of it — Aadhaar at 1.44 billion IDs, UPI at 21.70 billion transactions / US$308.45B in January 2026, DBT moving US$534.49B.
  • It buys citizen-data AI under the DPDP Act and the IndiaAI Mission's open-weight rule, so a foreign model on government citizen data is a security non-starter, not a pricing question (IBEF, citing PIB 9 Mar 2026).

Fintech & BFSI

the regulator asked for it

The regulator's own committee recommended a homegrown model

The opening
  • The strongest case anywhere: the regulator itself recommended it, so the regulator creates the demand and price does not decide it.
  • The RBI FREE-AI Committee Report (13 Aug 2025), Recommendation 4 calls for an indigenous, finance-specific AI model plus a way to fund it, citing data sovereignty and "excessive global LLM reliance".
  • This sits on top of RBI's binding 2018 rule that payments data stay in India.
  • About 79% of regulated firms are not yet using or developing AI (KPMG digest of the RBI report; structure corroborated by Dvara Research).

Health · Telecom · Energy · Defence · Transport

conditional · one opening each

Narrower plays — enter only where each rule set requires it

The opening per sector
  • Health — most sensitive data class under the DPDP Act 2023; ABDM is government-owned (≈797M ABHA accounts, ≈650M linked records), so inference must run in India (IndiaMedToday — MoHFW Lok Sabha, 28 Jul 2025).
  • Telecom & Energy — both legally Critical Information Infrastructure under IT Act S.70A (NCIIPC), so a foreign cloud model on call or grid data is barred (TRAI Yearly Performance Indicators 2024-25; Down To Earth — Grid India).
  • Defence — classified and air-gapped, with 75% of capital acquisition reserved for Indian industry (Inc42 — India's defence-AI reality check).
  • Transport & Logistics — Critical Information Infrastructure under IT Act S.70A (NCIIPC's critical-sector list names Transport); ports, aviation and the State-owned ULIP rail bar a foreign-cloud model, against a ≈US$286B logistics-cost pool (NCIIPC — critical sectors; Outlook Business — DPIIT-NCAER, logistics 7.97% of GDP).
  • The narrow point-solution niches are filling up; the only lasting way in is the model-and-software layer each rule set requires, not the whole sector.

The stack to build

The model layer is a crowded commodity; the chips are rented from one supplier and money alone cannot win there. Build the layers an outsider cannot copy; be honest about the one you rent.

Own the model — from scratch

the differentiator

Pretrain it · do not fine-tune a foreign base

The build
  • Fine-tuning a foreign base (Llama or Qwen) gives sovereign weights but ties you to someone else's model — what a "neither US nor China" pitch cannot honestly claim.
  • What you can defend is your own pretrained Indic family, the kind Sarvam ships: from-scratch Apache-2.0 Sarvam-1 (2B) and from-scratch sparse-MoE Sarvam-30B (~32B) / Sarvam-105B (~106B).
  • Sarvam states clearly that Sarvam-M (24B) is a Mistral post-train, not built from scratch (Sarvam — model family).

Own the data & the delivery

the durable layer

Indic corpora + the sovereign distribution layer

The build
  • The lasting advantage is language and regulation, not algorithms: 22 languages, voice-first low-literacy users, inference that runs in India under the DPDP Act.
  • Those rules rule out English-first frontier APIs by design.
  • What you build is the path from a model into a regulated, multilingual, government-bought deployment — on-prem serving, the Indic data and evaluation base, the integration work inside a ministry or a bank.
  • India can own this layer, and it will not build itself: the numbers do not add up until a mandate forces the deployment.

Rent the silicon — say so

conceded, not faked

The one layer sovereign money cannot buy

The build
  • No Indian player owns chips; compute ends at NVIDIA. The honest move is to rent it openly.
  • Sarvam runs on subsidised IndiaAI compute — 4,096 NVIDIA H100 for six months, ≈US$11.7M against a ≈US$29.4M project, the largest IndiaAI subsidy so far.
  • It does not pretend to own a stack it does not control (TICE — IndiaAI compute subsidy).
"Rented chips, so how is it sovereign?"
  • Sovereignty is not in the chips; it is in the controls, data and delivery layer on top of them.
  • Weights are owned inside the country, inference runs in India under the DPDP Act, and the deployment sits inside India's binding security regime — CERT-In, NCIIPC, MeitY empanelment, the 2018 payments-data rule.
  • Anyone can rent compute; the owned model, regulated-sector data and that control layer cannot be rented.
  • The warning cases — Krutrim, CoRover — collapsed own-model ambition into reselling cloud or a thin wrapper; here sovereignty means renting honestly and winning on the layers above.

02 · Origins & growth — global

Why sovereign AI became a thing

Sovereign AI did not grow like a normal market: from 2017 to 2022 no government funded an AI lab to avoid relying on another country. Then three dated triggers flipped it — five years of zero state funding became tens of billions in three, and the matrix below reads each trigger across (CSIS).

03 · The India imperative

Why India has no real choice but to run deployment sovereignty

  • Section 02 — Origins & growth left India one open path: own the language, data and citizen delivery, rent chips honestly.
  • India's funding curve is the opposite shape of the global one — flat 2020–2023, then one near-vertical jump.
  • That jump is a single Cabinet decision: the ≈US$1.23B IndiaAI Mission, approved 7 March 2024 (Elets eGov).
  • Yet only ≈US$47.7M was actually paid out in about two years (Medianama / Rajya Sabha reply).
  • India did not buy AI independence. It passed laws and paid subsidies to build it, the move it once used for hardware.

The same playbook, now applied to software

  • India's electronics playbook: subsidise the hard-to-get input, back Indian companies, require local content, buy first as the government.
  • The IndiaAI Mission runs that same playbook for software.
  • Read each row left to right: the lever, its hardware form, its sovereign-AI form, why the logic holds.

Pay for results, not for plans
  • Manufacturing PLI pays for results, not as an upfront grant.
  • A 4%–6% cash reward on extra India-made sales above a base year, for five years (ELCINA scheme text).
  • It worked: ≈US$2.4B paid out brought ≈US$20.3B investment and ≈US$190.9B output by March 2025 (India Briefing).
  • The software version exists: IndiaAI's Application Development Initiative pays in three stages.
  • ≤US$6K (₹5 lakh) prototype, ≤US$30K (₹25 lakh) ministry pilot, then ≤US$119K (₹1 crore) plus a nationwide-rollout contract and four years of support (IndiaAI IADI FAQ).
  • A startup is paid only after its AI is piloted and used inside a government system (IndiaAI).
  • PLI paid for units shipped, not factories that merely existed.
  • IADI pays for AI running in government, not a plan.
  • Money comes after the result.
Subsidise the input that is hard to get
  • PLI paid down what really held builders back: the cost of setting up electronics and chip plants in India.
  • The Mission does not buy GPUs.
  • It signs up private clouds and pays down GPU-time cost: 38,231 GPUs across 190 approved projects at ≈US$0.77/GPU-hour (≈₹65), as of 25 March 2026 (Newsgram).
  • That is roughly a third of the global cloud price (DQIndia).
  • GPU time is to AI builders what plant cost was to chip makers: the one thing a cash-light team cannot fund alone.
  • Cover it and a startup trains a top-tier model without a hyperscaler's balance sheet.
Back several Indian companies
  • PLI spread its rewards across a chosen group of Indian manufacturers, not one picked winner.
  • The Mission funds 12 organisations building foundation models.
  • Sarvam was picked first, 26 April 2025: a ≈US$11.7M compute subsidy on a ≈US$29.4M project, 4,096 H100 GPUs for six months via Yotta (TICE).
  • The 12-org cohort splits the allocation; the largest single grant is ≈US$126M total support (₹1,058.52 cr) (BharatGen; Medianama / Lok Sabha reply).
  • Same tool, software target: the government funds a group of national builders, not one bet.
  • It spreads money across companies, as PLI spread rewards across manufacturers.
Require that it be made in India
  • Local-content rules made the subsidy build something India owned, not a foreign assembly line on Indian soil.
  • Funded companies build national models from scratch and release the weights openly.
  • Sarvam built its own family (Sarvam-30B / 105B, Apache 2.0), launched 6 March 2026, on open AI4Bharat Indian-language data (Sarvam).
  • IndicVoices alone covers 12,000 hours across 22 languages (AI4Bharat IndicVoices).
  • Open weights plus your own training data are the software "made in India."
  • India owns the model; no foreign vendor can take it away by changing a licence.
Be the first big buyer, as the government
  • Government and state-owned company orders gave an Indian manufacturer a guaranteed buyer from day one.
  • Government demand is already real.
  • The Ministry of Agriculture's Digital Crop Survey ran for 400,000+ farmers across four states in October 2025 Company-reported (Sarvam).
  • SBI Life serves 80M+ customers in 11 languages Company-reported (Sarvam).
  • Tamil Nadu and Odisha signed state agreements (Sarvam).
  • Serving 1.4 billion citizens in 22 languages is a need no foreign lab meets on India's terms.
  • It gives Indian builders a guaranteed buyer, as orders made the PLI manufacturer safe to back.
Help industry grow, don't just regulate it
  • India built manufacturing strength with incentives and government orders, not market limits.
  • India has a data law, not an AI law. The March 2024 pre-launch permission order was pulled back within 15 days (AZB).
  • The India AI Governance Guidelines (5 Nov 2025) only say "do no harm", no standalone AI law (SCC Online).
  • DPDP Rules issued 14 Nov 2025, phase in by 14 May 2027 (Lexology / SAM).
  • India cannot out-build the others and will not win by adding rules.
  • It keeps options open by building, not restricting access — the opposite of the EU's regulate-access approach from Section 02 — Origins & growth.

  • Big powers treat AI as a national asset: the US sells the whole stack, the EU controls access.
  • A country that wants to stay independent cannot fully accept either.
  • The US "AI Action Plan" (23 Jul 2025) promises to sell "secure, full-stack AI export packages – including hardware, models, software, applications, and standards" to allies (White House).

  • India's curve is not a smaller global one — it is the opposite shape.
  • One near-vertical line from a single Cabinet approval, announced money far ahead of the ≈US$47.7M actually paid out.
  • That shape is the plan, not a weakness — the hardware PLI playbook run for software.
  • A builder is paid only after its AI is in use inside a government system.
  • The money math rules out every other option.
  • India cannot out-build the Gulf's sovereign-wealth campuses, nor own the chips almost no one outside China owns.
  • It cannot take the US full-stack package or EU rulebook without losing its independent position.
  • So this is not a wish, it is arithmetic.
  • The next question is not whether — it is who runs this path, and how well.

Owning a model is not sovereignty; you need enough layers around it that no one can switch it off. Asia's champions fine-tune a foreign base (SEA-LION on Qwen, Viettel on Llama, Typhoon from a Thai bank lab), and India repeats this.

  • Sarvam also owns the Indic data, live applications, and citizen and government delivery.
  • Sarvam holds its own pretrained model together with the data, application and citizen-delivery layers around it.

  • BharatGen ships Param-1 and Param2; Soket builds its own too (Harro / Medianama).
  • Sarvam ships Sarvam-1 and the Apache-2.0 from-scratch MoE Sarvam-30B/105B (Sarvam — 30B/105B; HF roster).

06 · The go-to-market

Distribution is the part India can win. The rules create the demand. The go-to-market makes it repeat.

Section 04 sets out which sectors are sovereign and how the rules shut a foreign model out. The practical question here: of the open stack layers, which can a low-capital country win and how is it sold — the answer is owning deployment, the path from a model to a regulated, multilingual, government-bought production system.

The layer India can win is distribution, not the model

Silicon, GPUs and the frontier model belong to better-funded US and Chinese labs, and no sovereign programme owns its silicon — Saudi Arabia’s HUMAIN still buys NVIDIA chips (NVIDIA, 13 May 2025). The one layer India can win is distribution: a sovereign model inside the channels, languages and regulated workflows through which 1.4 billion citizens get AI.

THE ASSET

State-owned distribution behind a language-and-law barrier

  • Aadhaar 1.44 billion IDs; UPI 21.70 billion transactions / US$308.45B (Jan 2026); DBT moving US$534.49B — reach no foreign vendor can copy (IBEF, citing PIB 9 Mar 2026).
  • Public service needs 22 languages, voice-first users and India-resident inference under DPDP; the open model is a commodity, so India can lose the model race and still own distribution.
  • Live anchors already run on State systems: 400,000+ farmers (MinAgri Digital Crop Survey, Sarvam — DCS); SBI Life 80 million+ in 11 languages (Sarvam — SBI Life) Company-reported.
→
THE CATCH

Required demand plus a repeatable way to integrate

  • Putting a model into health records, supervised banking or air-gapped defence is slow, committee-gated work the buyer cannot do alone.
  • Frontier labs chase API volume; the Indian buyer buys only when a rule requires sovereign-only or India-resident deployment.
  • Supply needs a repeatable way to fund the integration — and the IndiaAI cohort of 12 funded builders crowds the same procurements (Harro/Medianama mirror of MeitY Lok Sabha reply, 4 Apr 2026).

The go-to-market: pay on deployment, partner to deploy, own the engineers

A rule creates required demand but does not put a model into an air-gapped command post or a bank’s core, and India does not win this by selling AI the way US vendors sell SaaS. The go-to-market has three legs: an incentive that pays once sovereign AI is deployed; a partnership where the State deploys alongside you; and an in-house team that turns one pilot into a sector standard.

LEG 1

Pay on deployment

  • PLI pays 4–6% on incremental output, 5 years — not an upfront grant (ELCINA — Gazette 1 Apr 2020).
  • It returned US$20.3B investment and US$190.9B output for US$2.4B disbursed by Mar 2025 (India Briefing, 23 Jul 2025; IBEF, 14 Jul 2025).
  • The software version exists: IADI stage-gate ≈US$6.0K→US$29.8K→US$119K (₹5L→₹25L→₹1cr) + a nationwide deployment contract (IndiaAI — IADI FAQ IndiaAI (AWS S3) primary · local PDF extract; IndiaAI Challenge 2026).
  • Only ≈US$47.7M (₹400.94 cr) ≈ 3.9% released by 9 Feb 2026 — back-loaded like PLI (Harro/Medianama, 4 Apr 2026; RBI FREE-AI recommends a finance-model fund per Boston Institute of Analytics).
→
LEG 2

Partner to deploy

  • The data cannot leave the State’s network — RBI 2018, DPDP Rules, statutory CII, air-gap (RBI Notification, 6 Apr 2018).
  • The only delivery allowed is joint on-prem deployment, not a licence.
  • India’s contract forms: empanelment, a 36-month State panel (Business Standard, 17 Aug 2024; Inc42, Aug 2024); challenge-to-contract; RBI FREE-AI’s AI Innovation Sandbox (Dvara Research).
  • Sarvam runs on-prem GenAI for Aadhaar in 10 languages with UIDAI, plus Tamil Nadu and Odisha MoUs (UIDAI, 19 Mar 2025; Sarvam — state partnerships, Feb 2026).
→
LEG 3

Own the engineers

  • Palantir built a $4.475B FY2025 business (54% gov, 46% commercial, 954 customers) on the embedded team, not the software (Yahoo Finance, 3 Feb 2026; Palantir FY2025 10-K via StockTitan).
  • FDEs — ≈25% on-site / air-gapped, “one customer, many capabilities” — produce software economics: NDR 139%, top-20 avg $93.9M (+45%), 497→711→954, Rule-of-40 127 (The Pragmatic Engineer; Motley Fool, 2 Feb 2026).
  • Anthropic + Palantir + AWS put Claude into US defence at IL6 (TechCrunch, 7 Nov 2024); it grew to all 3 US gov branches, FedRAMP-High, a $200M DoD ceiling (Anthropic, 12 Aug 2025).
  • May 2026: Anthropic and OpenAI both launched FDE-model ventures — $1.5B and $10B. The delivery team is the missing piece and cannot be subcontracted (TechCrunch, 4 May 2026).

The four moves that make the go-to-market repeatable

MOVE 01

Government anchor first

  • Win the hardest, most-classified buyer — defence, the regulator, IndiaAI; switching is near-impossible.
  • In India the rule makes the anchor required, not sold: Sarvam was first selected to build the sovereign LLM (26 Apr 2025) on the Mission’s highest subsidy — ≈US$11.7M of a ≈US$29.4M project, 4,096 H100 for six months (Sarvam — India’s sovereign LLM; TICE, 16 Jul 2025; Business Today, 16 Mar 2026).
  • The defence / health / grid anchor is still a procurement to win.
→
MOVE 02

Forward-deployed integration

  • Embed engineers to own the air-gapped integration the buyer cannot do — from “it exists” to “it runs in production”.
  • In India: a team inside NHA/ABDM, a supervised bank, BSNL, a DISCOM or a Design Bureau, doing the on-prem work no system integrator will fund.
→
MOVE 03

Platform, not project

  • Each deployment hardens a reusable platform; the next customer buys a near-standard configuration, not a bespoke build.
  • The India shape is an Indic LLM + voice + VPC / air-gapped serving, spreading the unavoidable integration cost across the next bank, DISCOM or state.
→
MOVE 04

Anchor pulls the market

  • The government-grade credential and hardened platform pull regulated commercial buyers — Palantir’s commercial half grows faster after the anchor, not before.
  • The DPDP-compliant sovereign record is the only India-resident option where foreign frontier APIs are shut out.

Why the go-to-market builds on itself — a loop, not a one-time win

Land-and-expand inside the sovereign sectors is a loop that widens its own lead each turn: distribution is not a fixed asset to defend, it is the input to the next, harder-to-copy asset. Each stage must happen before the one below, and the last feeds back into the first.

01

Distribution reach

  • State channels + live anchors — 400,000+ farmers, SBI Life 80M+ — put a sovereign model before regulated users at a scale no foreign vendor reaches.
→
02

Forward-deployed

  • The in-house team drives the model into the air-gapped, India-resident workflow the buyer cannot integrate alone.
  • The cost the rule makes unavoidable is itself a barrier to anyone arriving later.
→
03

Regulated data & feedback

  • Working inside the workflow produces multilingual, sector-specific data that is legally resident, consent-bound and non-exportable — the asset frontier labs are barred from building here.
→
04

A model rivals can’t copy

  • That owned feedback tunes the in-country model on the exact tasks the sovereign buyer is judged on — a gap a data-excluded rival cannot close with compute alone.
→
05

Deeper lock-in

  • The hardened, sector-standard platform makes the next bank, DISCOM or state buy a near-standard configuration — widening reach and restarting the loop one step higher.
↺Stage 05 feeds back into Stage 01: distribution buys the data, the data buys the model, the model buys deeper distribution — each turn widens the lead.
  • The layer India can win is distribution: a frontier-grade open model is now a commodity, and the channels — citizen reach, the 22-language and India-resident requirements — are India’s and cannot be copied by a foreign vendor.
  • Imported silicon does not break this: compute is rented and swapped, but the owned model, the regulated-data feedback and the control layer cannot be — sovereignty lives in the control layer, not the chip foundry.
  • The demand comes from the rulebook: across the sovereign sectors the rules either shut the foreign model out or let it in only contained — turning demand you must persuade into demand that is required.
